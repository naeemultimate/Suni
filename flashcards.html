Peak Expiratory Flow Rate (PEFR) Measurement

Definition: A measure of how fast a person can exhale, indicating airway obstruction.

Key Steps:

  • Ensure patient is upright & relaxed
  • Attach disposable mouthpiece
  • Take a deep breath in
  • Blow out as hard & fast as possible
  • Record highest of three readings
  • Compare to personal best or predicted values

Errors to Avoid:

  • ❌ Poor effort/incomplete breath
  • ❌ Tongue blocking mouthpiece
  • ❌ Coughing during test

Management:

If PEFR is low → Adjust medication or seek medical review

Knee Examination (MSK)

Definition: A systematic approach to examining the knee joint for pathology.

Key Steps:

  • Introduce yourself & confirm patient details
  • Expose both legs & position the patient comfortably
  • Inspect for swelling, deformity, scars, or muscle wasting
  • Palpate joint line, patella, & popliteal fossa for tenderness
  • Assess range of motion (flexion, extension)
  • Special tests: Lachman’s (ACL), McMurray’s (meniscus), Valgus/Varus stress (MCL/LCL)

Key Findings:

  • Joint effusion → Consider arthritis, trauma
  • Positive McMurray’s → Meniscal tear
  • Anterior translation on Lachman’s → ACL rupture

Cranial Nerve Examination

Definition: A neurological assessment of the 12 cranial nerves.

Key Steps:

  • Olfactory (I): Smell test
  • Optic (II): Visual acuity, fields, & pupillary reflex
  • Oculomotor, Trochlear, Abducens (III, IV, VI): Eye movements
  • Trigeminal (V): Facial sensation & jaw movements
  • Facial (VII): Facial expression & taste
  • Vestibulocochlear (VIII): Hearing & balance
  • Glossopharyngeal, Vagus (IX, X): Gag reflex, voice changes
  • Accessory (XI): Shoulder shrug
  • Hypoglossal (XII): Tongue movement

Key Findings:

  • Ptosis & pupil dilation → CN III palsy
  • Unilateral hearing loss → CN VIII dysfunction
  • Deviation of tongue → CN XII lesion

Thyroid Status Examination

Definition: A clinical examination to assess for signs of hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

Key Steps:

  • General Inspection: Observe for weight changes, agitation, sweating, or dry skin.
  • Hands & Arms: Check for tremor (hyperthyroid), cold/dry skin (hypothyroid), palmar erythema, and pulse rate.
  • Face: Assess for periorbital swelling (hypothyroid), exophthalmos & lid lag (hyperthyroid).
  • Neck Examination:
    • Inspect for thyroid enlargement, scars, or asymmetry.
    • Palpate thyroid from behind, checking for size, consistency, nodules.
    • Auscultate for a thyroid bruit (suggests hyperthyroidism).
  • Special Tests:
    • Reflexes – brisk (hyperthyroid) or slow (hypothyroid).
    • Pretibial Myxoedema – associated with Graves' disease.
    • Proximal Muscle Weakness – test shoulder abduction against resistance.

Key Findings:

  • Hyperthyroid Signs: Tachycardia, weight loss, fine tremor, lid lag, exophthalmos.
  • Hypothyroid Signs: Bradycardia, weight gain, cold intolerance, dry skin, slow reflexes.
  • Goitre: Consider iodine deficiency, Graves' disease, or nodular thyroid disease.
