Peak Expiratory Flow Rate (PEFR) Measurement

Definition: A measure of how fast a person can exhale, indicating airway obstruction.

Key Steps:

  • Ensure patient is upright & relaxed
  • Attach disposable mouthpiece
  • Take a deep breath in
  • Blow out as hard & fast as possible
  • Record highest of three readings
  • Compare to personal best or predicted values

Errors to Avoid:

  • ❌ Poor effort/incomplete breath
  • ❌ Tongue blocking mouthpiece
  • ❌ Coughing during test

Management:

If PEFR is low → Adjust medication or seek medical review

Knee Examination (MSK)

Definition: A systematic approach to examining the knee joint for pathology.

Key Steps:

  • Introduce yourself & confirm patient details
  • Expose both legs & position the patient comfortably
  • Inspect for swelling, deformity, scars, or muscle wasting
  • Palpate joint line, patella, & popliteal fossa for tenderness
  • Assess range of motion (flexion, extension)
  • Special tests: Lachman’s (ACL), McMurray’s (meniscus), Valgus/Varus stress (MCL/LCL)

Key Findings:

  • Joint effusion → Consider arthritis, trauma
  • Positive McMurray’s → Meniscal tear
  • Anterior translation on Lachman’s → ACL rupture

Cranial Nerve Examination

Definition: A neurological assessment of the 12 cranial nerves.

Key Steps:

  • Olfactory (I): Smell test
  • Optic (II): Visual acuity, fields, & pupillary reflex
  • Oculomotor, Trochlear, Abducens (III, IV, VI): Eye movements
  • Trigeminal (V): Facial sensation & jaw movements
  • Facial (VII): Facial expression & taste
  • Vestibulocochlear (VIII): Hearing & balance
  • Glossopharyngeal, Vagus (IX, X): Gag reflex, voice changes
  • Accessory (XI): Shoulder shrug
  • Hypoglossal (XII): Tongue movement

Key Findings:

  • Ptosis & pupil dilation → CN III palsy
  • Unilateral hearing loss → CN VIII dysfunction
  • Deviation of tongue → CN XII lesion

Thyroid Status Examination

Definition: A clinical examination to assess for signs of hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

Key Steps:

  • General Inspection: Observe for weight changes, agitation, sweating, or dry skin.
  • Hands & Arms: Check for tremor (hyperthyroid), cold/dry skin (hypothyroid), palmar erythema, and pulse rate.
  • Face: Assess for periorbital swelling (hypothyroid), exophthalmos & lid lag (hyperthyroid).
  • Neck Examination:
    • Inspect for thyroid enlargement, scars, or asymmetry.
    • Palpate thyroid from behind, checking for size, consistency, nodules.
    • Auscultate for a thyroid bruit (suggests hyperthyroidism).
  • Special Tests:
    • Reflexes – brisk (hyperthyroid) or slow (hypothyroid).
    • Pretibial Myxoedema – associated with Graves' disease.
    • Proximal Muscle Weakness – test shoulder abduction against resistance.

Key Findings:

  • Hyperthyroid Signs: Tachycardia, weight loss, fine tremor, lid lag, exophthalmos.
  • Hypothyroid Signs: Bradycardia, weight gain, cold intolerance, dry skin, slow reflexes.
  • Goitre: Consider iodine deficiency, Graves' disease, or nodular thyroid disease.

Elbow Examination

Definition: A musculoskeletal assessment of the elbow joint to identify pathology such as arthritis, fractures, or ligament injuries.

Key Steps:

  • General Inspection: Check for swelling, erythema, deformity, scars, muscle wasting.
  • Palpation:
    • Assess for tenderness along the joint line, olecranon, medial and lateral epicondyles.
    • Check for effusion (boggy feel around the joint).
  • Range of Motion:
    • Flexion (C5/6) and Extension (C7).
    • Pronation and Supination (C6). Compare with the other arm.
  • Special Tests:
    • Resisted wrist extension – Lateral epicondylitis (Tennis elbow).
    • Resisted wrist flexion – Medial epicondylitis (Golfer’s elbow).
    • Tinel’s test – Ulnar nerve irritation at the cubital tunnel.

Key Findings:

  • Pain on resisted wrist extension → Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis).
  • Pain on resisted wrist flexion → Golfer’s elbow (medial epicondylitis).
  • Restricted movement → Arthritis, joint effusion.
  • Nerve involvement (ulnar, radial) → Weakness, sensory loss.

Upper Limb Neurological Examination

Definition: A neurological assessment of motor and sensory function in the upper limbs.

Key Steps:

  • General Inspection: Look for muscle wasting, fasciculations, tremors, scars.
  • Tone: Assess passive movement for hypertonia (spasticity, rigidity) or hypotonia.
  • Power: Test muscle strength for key movements:
    • Shoulder abduction and adduction
    • Elbow flexion and extension
    • Wrist extension and flexion
    • Finger extension, flexion, abduction
  • Reflexes: Test biceps (C5/6), triceps (C7), supinator (C6) reflexes.
  • Sensation: Assess light touch, pinprick, vibration and proprioception.
  • Coordination: Finger-to-nose test, rapid alternating movements.

Key Findings:

  • Hyperreflexia → Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) lesion
  • Hyporeflexia → Lower Motor Neuron (LMN) lesion
  • Weakness with fasciculations → LMN pathology
  • Intention tremor → Cerebellar disorder

Shoulder Examination

Definition: A musculoskeletal assessment of the shoulder joint to identify pathology such as rotator cuff injury, arthritis, or instability.

Key Steps:

  • General Inspection: Look for asymmetry, swelling, muscle wasting, scars, bruising.
  • Palpation:
    • Feel for tenderness at the sternoclavicular joint, acromioclavicular joint, and glenohumeral joint.
    • Palpate the rotator cuff tendons and long head of the biceps tendon.
  • Range of Motion:
    • Flexion and extension
    • Abduction and adduction
    • Internal and external rotation
  • Special Tests:
    • Neer’s test – Subacromial impingement.
    • Hawkins-Kennedy test – Subacromial impingement.
    • Empty Can test – Supraspinatus tear.
    • Lift-Off test – Subscapularis weakness.
    • Apprehension test – Shoulder instability.

Key Findings:

  • Reduced range of motion → Frozen shoulder or arthritis.
  • Positive Empty Can test → Supraspinatus pathology.
  • Positive Hawkins-Kennedy → Subacromial impingement.
  • Positive Apprehension test → Shoulder instability or dislocation history.
